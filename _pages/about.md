# Bridging Complex Network Physics & Advanced Computation

I am a PhD Candidate at the **University of Liège**, specializing in the physics of **silver nanowire networks** and **materials for energy**. My work sits at the intersection of experimental nanostructured physics and high-performance numerical optimization.

## 🔬 Research Focus: The Physics of Connectivity
My research investigates how local junctions dictate global properties in disordered systems. 

* **Silver Nanowire Networks:** Studying the percolation thresholds, optical transparency, and charge transport using a "Lab-to-Code" approach.
* **Thermochromic Materials:** Numerical modeling of phase-transition materials for smart-window energy efficiency.
* **Methodology:** A full-stack workflow—from **RF Sputtering** and **SEM Imaging** to **HPC-scale simulations** (OpenMP/MPI) on supercomputers.

---

## 💻 The Algorithmic Edge
Beyond the lab, I am driven by computational efficiency and system optimization.

* **Computational Physics:** Fluent in **C++, Python, and Mathematica**. I specialize in writing optimized solvers for transport in random networks that leverage supercomputing clusters.
* **Competitive Programming:** 3-time competition winner. I bring the rigor of algorithmic speed to physical simulations.
* **Tech Leadership:** Co-founder of a HealthTech startup. I apply **global optimization algorithms** (developed during my PhD) to solve complex hospital logistics and medical planning.
